Hard Bot Specification 

Design and implement a “Hard Difficulty” bot for a turn-based, least-score card game. The bot must simulate intelligent, human-like play using memory, probabilistic reasoning, opponent modeling, and risk-aware decision-making. The bot should not be perfectly optimal; controlled imperfections must be introduced to maintain fairness and realism.

---

## 1. Core Objectives

The bot must:

* Minimize its final hand score.
* Form optimal card combinations (pairs, sequences, flushes as per rules).
* Decide between drawing from visible discard or hidden deck using expected value.
* Track known cards and infer unseen cards probabilistically.
* Estimate opponent hand strength using observed behavior.
* Declare strategically based on quantified risk.
* Avoid helping opponents through poor discards.

---

## 2. Card Memory System

Maintain a dynamic knowledge base of cards:

* `seenCards`: All revealed cards (drawn + discarded)
* `discardHistory`: Ordered list of discarded cards
* `remainingDeckDistribution`: Probability distribution of unseen cards

From this, compute:

* Probability of drawing any specific card from the deck
* Remaining count of each rank/suit

---

## 3. Hand Optimization Engine

At every turn, compute the optimal grouping of the bot’s hand.

Goal:

* Minimize total score after forming valid combinations

Constraints:

* Respect game rules (valid sequences, pairs, etc.)

Approach:

* Use heuristic optimization (not brute force):

  * Prioritize longer sequences over smaller sets
  * Prefer combinations that reduce high-value cards
  * Recalculate after every draw/discard

---

## 4. Draw Decision Logic (Expected Value Model)

When choosing between:

* Visible discard card (`C_visible`)
* Hidden deck draw

### Step 1: Evaluate Visible Card

* Compute immediate score improvement if taken
* Check if it completes or improves a combination

### Step 2: Evaluate Hidden Draw (Expected Value)

For all possible unseen cards:

* Compute:
  Expected Gain = Σ [P(card) × score_improvement(card)]

Where:

* P(card) = probability from remainingDeckDistribution
* score_improvement(card) = reduction in hand score after optimal regrouping

### Decision Rule:

* Choose visible card if:
  guaranteed_gain_visible > expected_gain_hidden
* Else draw from deck

---

## 5. Opponent Modeling System

For each opponent, maintain:

* Cards discarded (frequency and pattern)
* Suits avoided or preferred
* Pickup behavior (what they choose to draw from discard)

Infer:

* Likely combinations they are building
* Suits/ranks they do not need
* Estimated score range

Represent opponent score as:

* `estimatedScoreRange = [min_possible, max_possible]`

---

## 6. Discard Strategy

When discarding a card:

Avoid:

* Cards likely useful to opponents based on their behavior

Prefer:

* High-value cards not contributing to bot’s combinations
* Cards from suits opponents are avoiding

---

## 7. Declaration Logic (Risk-Based)

At each turn, evaluate whether to declare.

### Step 1:

Compute bot score → `S_bot`

### Step 2:

For each opponent:
Estimate probability:

* `P(opponent_score < S_bot)`

### Step 3:

Compute overall risk:

* `Risk = probability that at least one opponent has a lower score`

### Step 4: Decision Rule

Bot declares if:

* `Risk < threshold` (recommended: 15%–25%)
  AND
* Expected improvement from waiting is negligible

---

## 8. Controlled Imperfection (Humanization Layer)

To avoid perfect play:

Introduce:

* 5–10% probability of suboptimal decisions
* Slight noise in probability calculations
* Occasional delayed declaration

This ensures:

* Realistic gameplay
* Reduced player frustration

---

## 9. Difficulty Calibration Parameters

Expose tunable parameters:

* Risk threshold (e.g., 0.15–0.25)
* Memory accuracy (100% for hard)
* Decision noise factor
* Aggression level (early vs late declaration)

---

## 10. Performance Constraints

* All decisions must complete within real-time limits (<100ms preferred)
* Avoid brute-force combinatorics where possible
* Use cached evaluations where applicable

---

## 11. Summary Behavior

A Hard Bot should:

* Play consistently strong but not perfect
* Make statistically sound decisions
* Adapt to opponents
* Take calculated risks
* Avoid obvious mistakes
* Feel human-like rather than mechanical

Easy Bot Specification

Design and implement an “Easy Difficulty” bot for a turn-based, least-score card game. The bot should simulate a beginner-level human player: it understands the rules and basic strategies but plays inconsistently, makes frequent mistakes, and lacks long-term planning. The goal is to create a bot that is **beatable, slightly unpredictable, and still believable**.

---

## 1. Core Objectives

The bot must:

* Attempt to minimize its hand score using simple strategies.
* Form obvious combinations (pairs, sequences, etc.).
* Prefer safe and straightforward decisions over optimal ones.
* Exhibit inconsistent behavior via controlled mistakes.
* Avoid advanced reasoning such as probability calculation or deep opponent modeling.

---

## 2. Memory System (Very Limited)

Maintain minimal short-term memory:

* `recentDiscards`: Last 2–3 cards discarded by any player
* `selfPickedCards`: Cards the bot picked from discard pile

The bot must NOT:

* Track full deck composition
* Estimate probabilities of unseen cards
* Maintain long-term memory

---

## 3. Hand Evaluation Logic (Greedy)

At each turn:

* Identify obvious valid combinations (pairs, sequences, flushes)
* Keep combinations that immediately reduce score
* Do not restructure hand for future optimization

Limitations:

* Does not evaluate multiple grouping strategies deeply
* May keep suboptimal combinations
* May fail to break weak combos for better outcomes

---

## 4. Draw Decision Logic (Rule-Based)

When choosing between:

* Visible discard card (`C_visible`)
* Hidden deck draw

### Decision Rules:

Take visible card if:

* It directly completes a valid combination
  OR
* It replaces a high-value card (≥ 8) and reduces total score

Otherwise:

* Draw from hidden deck

### Imperfection Layer:

* 20% probability to ignore a beneficial visible card and draw from deck instead

---

## 5. Discard Strategy (Semi-Naive)

When discarding:

1. Identify cards not part of any combination
2. Select highest-value card among them

### Minor Awareness:

* Avoid discarding a card that matches one of the last 1–2 recent opponent picks (if present in memory)

Limitations:

* Does not predict opponent needs
* Does not evaluate future utility of cards

---

## 6. Opponent Awareness (Minimal)

* If an opponent picks the same suit multiple times within recent memory:

  * Slightly reduce probability of discarding cards of that suit

Implementation:

* Apply low-weight bias only (do not override discard logic)

The bot must NOT:

* Estimate opponent score
* Infer complex strategies
* Track long-term behavior

---

## 7. Declaration Logic (Safe but Inconsistent)

Primary rule:

* Declare when:

  ```
  hand_score ≤ 6–8
  ```

### Imperfection Layer:

* 20% probability to declare early (hand_score ≤ 10)
* 20% probability to delay declaration even when threshold is met

The bot does not:

* Estimate opponent scores
* Calculate declaration risk

---

## 8. Mistake Injection System

Introduce controlled randomness to simulate human error:

* 10–20% probability:

  * Suboptimal discard choice
* 10–15% probability:

  * Poor draw decision (ignore visible benefit)
* Occasional:

  * Retain a high-value or useless card longer than optimal

Mistakes should:

* Be small and plausible
* Not completely break gameplay logic

---

## 10. Performance Constraints

* Avoid heavy computation or combinatorial search
* Prefer rule-based evaluation over optimization algorithms

---

## 11. Behavioral Summary

The Easy Bot should:

* Play correctly but not optimally
* Miss obvious opportunities occasionally
* Make inconsistent decisions
* React to the current state without planning ahead
* Feel like a beginner-level human player

---

## 12. Difficulty Characteristics

* High error rate compared to advanced bots
* Low strategic depth
* Predictable core behavior with slight randomness
* Designed to be beaten by most players with basic understanding
